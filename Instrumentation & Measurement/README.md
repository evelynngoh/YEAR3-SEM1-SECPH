Measurement and Instrumentation course provided valuable exposure to the principles of electrical measurements, instrumentation systems, transducers, signal conditioning, and data acquisition techniques used in engineering applications. Throughout the course, understanding of measurement accuracy, error analysis, calibration, and electrical measuring instruments was strengthened through both theoretical learning and practical laboratory activities. Hands-on exercises and project-based learning activities improved technical competency in analysing measurement data, selecting suitable instruments, and applying signal processing methods for electrical measurement systems. In addition, the course enhanced analytical thinking, problem-solving, teamwork, and technical reporting skills through laboratory experiments, assignments, and group projects. Exposure to instrumentation tools, sensors, and data acquisition systems also increased understanding of how precise measurement and monitoring systems are applied in real industrial and engineering environments. Overall, this elective course successfully broadened technical knowledge and practical skills related to instrumentation and measurement technologies.
